Team Reflection

Having looked at the Burning Glass data, has your opinion of your ideal job changed? Why/Why not?

Catt:

After carefully reviewing my listed skills in the Burning Glass Rank, I would still affirm that my ideal job has not been altered and I will always try my best to progress my future career to the Senior Systems Administrator. Moreover, IT is my passion so I would love to be the one who helps others with their problems/complications in the IT field. Additionally, if I follow my passion for IT now, it will eventually lead me to the progressively level path in the IT field career shortly. Besides, considering that I have already acquired some of the very important skills such as organisational skills, problem-solving skills, and attention to detail, these can be exceptionally supportive while combining both soft and hard skills, along with my passion, and how much I will enjoy, I believe that is the key for the first step to finding my dream job. As a result, it motivates me to learn and study Information Technology with the intention that I can be anything I want and do whatever I am keen to do in the future.

Liz:

My opinion of my chosen ideal job (Front End Developer) hasn’t really changed. I think there will always be a need for this role even if it is not the #1 in-demand job. I am already on my way to gaining several of the required skill both generic and tech-specific and think it would be a good fit. However, while this seems like an easy choice for me, I have interests in other areas as well that I would like to explore. Cloud security is one that I am interested to learn more about, even though it wasn’t initially in the 2018 data reports top 25, more recent predictions have shown cloud-related roles, such as cloud security and cloud architect gaining higher demand, and from what I have read recently, it is going to become more important still as many businesses have been making the move to cloud services since the beginning of the pandemic. It also appears that women are heavily under-represented in that sector, meaning that more opportunities would arise for me from organisations that are looking to create inclusive teams, which is the kind of environment I would want to work in no matter which role I aspire to.

Sam:

Yes and No. I still hold that the job I’m working towards will give me the outcomes and fulfilment I’m looking for. However, after looking at the data I start to wonder what I’m missing out on by only focusing on a singular pathway. For me, my ideal job has not changed, but rather the definition of what is ideal for myself has shifted and I’m beginning to plan a longer career pathway into opportunities past the mid/senior software engineer in game design hopefully leading to more responsibility, opportunity and reward.
